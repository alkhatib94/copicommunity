# Episode 94 - <span className="text-orange-500">Live Nodes!</span>  

In COPICafe Episode 94 titled *"Live Nodes!"*, hosts Josh Jones and Rob Greig discuss the latest developments in **Cornucopias**, including their experiences at **Consensus 2024**, new industry partnerships, and technical progress on **nodes and infrastructure**.  

</br>  

Highlights include updates on the **delegate system for nodes**, details on **rarity mechanics** within the game, and the upcoming release of a **nodes calculator**. The team emphasizes professionalism, industry engagement, and their long-term strategy to bridge mainstream and blockchain gaming.  

</br>  

[00:00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=0) The hosts recap **Consensus 2024**, discussing their booth, land and node updates, ISPO block production, and community questions. They stress the importance of in-person events for building connections and gathering reactions from new players.  

</br>  

[00:05:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=300) Reflections on **Consensus 2024** interactions. Attendees were surprised Cornucopias is blockchain-based, highlighting its unique niche in Web3 gaming. The team predicts future conferences will spotlight blockchain gaming and sees Cornucopias as a gateway for mainstream players.  

</br>  

[00:10:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=600) Engagement with industry professionals and influencers, including **Star Citizen’s founder**, a **game economist**, George from *CryptosRUs*, and Jesus Martinez. The event demonstrates gaming’s ability to create meaningful industry connections.  

</br>  

[00:15:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=900) Event highlights include discovering a **DeLorean car** and meeting **Rampage’s original developer**, Kenneth Hurley. The team explored HR solutions, financial products, and potential partnerships, showing the broad applications of blockchain and Web3.  

</br>  

[00:20:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=1200) Announcement of a **potential partnership with Apex Fusion**, a cross-chain solution for Cardano. This collaboration will help Cardano projects generate revenue and survive the bear cycle. Updates also cover **World Travel Loop** progress and Land Grab tasks.  

</br>  

[00:25:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=1500) Excitement about the game’s **water technology**, vital for fishing and exploration. Progress on **file nodes** continues, with public access coming soon. While setup currently requires technical skill, improvements for ease-of-use are planned.  

</br>  

[00:30:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=1800) Development of the **delegate system for nodes**. Linux and Windows versions will be available via Docker. Current node holders get priority on future nodes. Rollout occurs in three phases:  
1. Rewards begin July 1, 2024  
2. Public downloads  
3. Performance-based rewards.  

</br>  

[00:35:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=2100) Node updates: delegate count doubled from **2 → 4 per node**. Public pools can scale delegates significantly. Rewards will be split between **Cornucopias** and a **delegate subscription service**. A website dashboard for reward claims is in development.  

</br>  

[00:40:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=2400) Partnership with **DripDropz** to streamline claiming. A **nodes calculator** proof of concept (via ChatGPT) will launch soon. Discussion of **land rarity** mechanics, which affect resources, XP boosts, and maintenance, while keeping gameplay fair.  

</br>  

[00:45:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=2700) Deep dive into **rarity mechanics**:  
- **Mythic → Legendary → Rare → Uncommon → Common** determines land plot selection order.  
- Aesthetics differ by rarity for visual variety.  
- Lower rarities provide more XP boosts but require more maintenance.  

</br>  

[00:50:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-SxyBOg1f0&t=3000) Episode wrap-up. The hosts thank the community, express excitement for the future of Cornucopias, and sign off until next week.
