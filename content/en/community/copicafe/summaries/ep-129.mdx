# Episode 129 - The Elysian  

### Summary  

Cornucopias is a cutting-edge open-world MMO set in a futuristic sky-world composed of floating islands enclosed in advanced domes. In this episode, Rob Greig, Josh Jones, and David share development progress and updates, including build improvements, the upgrade to Unreal Engine 5.6, community feedback on governance and land-moving systems, tokenomics updates for $COPI and IGC, cloud gaming support, and a sneak peek at the new player onboarding experience through the Elysian ship and navigation features.  

</br>

David Pew provides insights into the complexity of Avatar 3.0 and combat systems, highlighting the technical challenges and achievements. The team emphasizes the importance of community input in shaping governance and land-moving mechanics while ensuring fairness and balance. The episode also covers upcoming marketplace enhancements and previews exciting future gameplay and social features that set the stage for Cornucopias’ next growth phase.  

</br>

## Key Insights  

<ul className="styled-list">
  <li>🔧 <strong>Avatar and Combat Systems Complexity:</strong><br/>  
  Avatar 3.0 required thousands of tasks-morphs, bones, animations-handled by a small but dedicated team. Combat development, especially reload mechanics with motion capture, shows the technical ambition for immersive, AAA-quality gameplay.</li>

</br>

  <li>⚡ <strong>Unreal Engine 5.6 Impact:</strong><br/>  
  The early release of UE 5.6 brings 20-30% performance boosts through GPU and rendering optimizations, reducing frame drops and improving stability across devices, especially mid-range rigs.</li>

</br>

  <li>🏙️ <strong>Governance Mechanics with Player Collaboration:</strong><br/>  
  Community-driven town governance empowers collective decision-making while ensuring no single player can negatively impact others. This approach strengthens cooperation and long-term community identity.</li>

</br>

  <li>🔄 <strong>Land-Moving with Seasonal Constraints:</strong><br/>  
  Allowing players to move land plots introduces flexibility and addresses “dead land” concerns. Seasonal restrictions balance freedom with economic and gameplay stability, adding strategic depth.</li>

</br>

  <li>💸 <strong>Dual Tokenomics for Stability:</strong><br/>  
  Separating $COPI (premium currency) from IGC (in-game earnings) builds a sustainable economy and prevents external market manipulation. Expert oversight ensures robust long-term growth and utility.</li>

</br>

  <li>☁️ <strong>Cloud Gaming as a Growth Strategy:</strong><br/>  
  Planned integration with services like GeForce Now expands access to high-quality gameplay on low-end devices, aligning Cornucopias with the industry’s move toward streaming-based gaming.</li>

</br>

  <li>🗺️ <strong>Improved Onboarding with the Elysian Ship:</strong><br/>  
  The new player journey begins on the luxury Alleion/Elysian ship, providing orientation and a narrative-rich introduction. Dynamic maps with points of interest enhance navigation and reduce confusion.</li>
</ul>  

</br>

## Conclusion   

Episode 129 of COPI Café delivers a forward-looking update on Cornucopias’ technical, economic, and community development. With advancements in Avatar 3.0, combat, and Unreal Engine 5.6, the team shows its ambition to deliver a polished AAA experience. Together, these elements reflect Cornucopias’ dedication to building a scalable, immersive, and community-driven metaverse.
